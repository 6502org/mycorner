I2C Bus. By Lee Davison.
Introduction.

On the top right hand side of the board is a four pin connector labeled LED DISPLAYS and numbered as P4.

This connector was used as an I2C bus port and has the necessary open collector drivers and pull up resistors, all that's needed is some software.

I2C Bus hardware.

For the purpose of having something to drive a small board was made up with a RAMTROM FM30C256 'data collector' chip. This chip has 32k bytes of non volatile RAM, a real time clock, a reset generator (not used in this application) and a tamper detector input which is active even when the system is powered down.
